= 1. Introduction and Goals

== 1.1 Requirements Overview

Magrathea PKI is a product for building and operating public-key infrastructure and trust-management capabilities.

The product supports protocol-specific PKI domains without collapsing them into a generic certificate model:

* X.509 certificate authority and lifecycle capabilities;
* CVC certificate capabilities;
* SSH certificate capabilities.

The delivery roadmap starts with X.509, then adds CVC, then SSH.

Magrathea PKI supports multiple key custody and trust profiles:

* software-based key custody;
* optional HSM-backed key custody;
* optional TPM-backed trust and attestation profiles.

When HSM or TPM capabilities are product-owned or product-managed, they are inside the Magrathea PKI solution boundary. Their physical placement is described in the Deployment View.

The architecture follows DDD and BDD-first development. C4 containers represent deployable bounded contexts, not packaging artifacts.

Shared kernels provide common capabilities where appropriate:

* Shared PKI Kernel;
* Crypto/Key Custody Kernel;
* Policy Kernel;
* Audit Kernel.

Compliance targets include FIPS, eIDAS, ETSI, and Common Criteria.

== 1.2 Quality Goals

[cols="1,2",options="header"]
|===
| Priority | Quality Goal

| 1
| Security: protect keys, trust decisions, certificate lifecycle operations, and administrative access.

| 2
| Correctness: enforce PKI rules, policies, certificate semantics, and lifecycle transitions accurately.

| 3
| Auditability: provide reliable evidence for security, compliance, and operational review.

| 4
| Modularity: keep X.509, CVC, SSH, custody, policy, and audit concerns independently evolvable.

| 5
| Testability: validate requirements through BDD-first executable specifications and focused implementation tests.

| 6
| Operability: support reliable product operation, diagnostics, and deployment across supported trust profiles.
|===

|===

| Product owner
| Delivers a commercially useful PKI product with a clear roadmap and extensible trust capabilities.

| Security architect
| Ensures strong key protection, policy enforcement, and secure trust boundaries.

| PKI architect
| Ensures protocol-specific correctness for X.509, CVC, and SSH PKI domains.

| Performs registration and identity-verification workflows reliably and traceably.

| CA operator
| Operates certification authority workflows securely and according to policy.

| Verifies evidence for FIPS, eIDAS, ETSI, Common Criteria, and internal controls.

| Needs clear bounded contexts, executable requirements, and maintainable architecture.

| Operator
| Needs observable, deployable, and supportable runtime components.
